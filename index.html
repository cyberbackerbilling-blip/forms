Rigorous Vetting
Good to Great — Connection

Collins distinguishes rigorous from ruthless. Ruthless cuts indiscriminately. Rigorous applies exacting standards consistently. The <1% standard is not a rejection rate — it is a commitment to never lowering the bar when the pipeline is thin.

Every compromise here compounds downstream: a wrong placement stresses the client, costs the team, and drags the flywheel backward.

Character is harder to teach than skill — but skill still matters. CBU and the skill rating system convert raw talent into deployable, rated, client-ready professionals. Without this node, placement becomes guesswork and client satisfaction becomes luck.

Node Four
Tiered Placement
Good to Great — Connection

Collins calls this the right people in the right seats. A Cyberbacker who is a good match but not a great match will eventually churn — on the client side or the CB side. Tiered placement means we do not just fill seats. We engineer fit.

A mismatch here creates downstream collections risk, replacement cost, and a trust deficit with the client.

"One would be people. Two would be people. Three would be people. A huge part of our transition can be attributed to our discipline in picking the right people." — Circuit City VP, Good to Great

Value Appreciation
Good to Great — Connection

The flywheel does not spin from a single push — it builds from consistent, compounding effort. Value appreciation is how Cyberbacker converts a satisfied client into an expanding one.

If clients don't feel the Cyberbacker growing, they plateau. If CBs don't feel their value recognized, they disengage. Both sides of the relationship must feel the momentum.

"Good-to-great transformations never happened in one fell swoop. The flywheel builds momentum, eventually hitting a point of breakthrough." — Jim Collins
